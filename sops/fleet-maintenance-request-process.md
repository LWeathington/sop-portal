# Fleet Maintenance Request Process

**Department:** Fleet Maintenance
**Owner:** Mike Jackson — Fleet Maintenance Manager
**Supports:** Lindsay Weathington (spend approval + paperwork)
**Effective:** April 2026

---

## Overview

This SOP defines how all fleet maintenance and repair requests are handled at Spot On Logistics. Mike Jackson owns this process end-to-end. Nothing goes to a shop without going through this process.

---

## Step 1: Issue Identification

Issues can be identified by anyone on the team:

- **Driver** — reports via DVIR in Samsara or direct call/text to Austin
- **Austin** — observes during pre-trip check or receives driver complaint
- **Phil** — observes issue at KC yard
- **Mike** — identifies during routine inspection or Samsara fault review

**All issues must be reported to Mike Jackson.** Austin or Phil should never schedule repairs directly.

---

## Step 2: Mike Evaluates the Issue

Mike will:
1. Confirm the issue (inspect in person or review Samsara DVIR/fault codes)
2. Determine urgency:
   - **Safety issue** → truck pulled immediately, not dispatched until resolved
   - **Non-safety issue** → schedule repair at next available opportunity
3. Contact preferred vendor or shop for availability and quote

---

## Step 3: Spend Approval

| Estimated Cost | Approval Required | Action |
|----------------|------------------|--------|
| Under $500 | None — Mike auto-approves | Proceed to Step 4 |
| $500 – $2,500 | Lindsay Weathington | Mike contacts Lindsay for cash flow check before authorizing |
| Over $2,500 | Blake Lappan | Mike contacts Blake with quote and recommendation |

**For Lindsay approval:** Mike texts or emails Lindsay with:
- Truck number
- Issue description
- Shop name
- Estimated cost and timeline

**For Blake approval:** Mike presents using the 131 Rule — 1 Problem, 3 Solutions, 1 Recommendation.

---

## Step 4: Schedule the Repair

Once approved:
1. Mike contacts the shop to schedule drop-off
2. Mike coordinates with Austin to arrange driver drop-off if needed
3. Mike confirms expected completion date
4. Mike notifies Austin of any truck that will be out of service and the expected return date

---

## Step 5: Repair Completion

When the repair is complete:
1. Mike picks up vehicle or arranges driver pick-up with Austin
2. Mike obtains the repair invoice/receipt from the shop
3. Mike sends invoice and repair summary to Lindsay via email or text

---

## Step 6: Documentation (Lindsay's Role)

Lindsay will:
1. File the repair invoice in the vehicle's compliance folder
2. Update the fleet maintenance log
3. Process the payment to the vendor in QuickBooks
4. Flag if any tags, registrations, or compliance docs need to be updated as a result

---

## Emergency Repairs

If a truck breaks down on the road:
1. Driver contacts Austin immediately
2. Austin contacts Mike
3. Mike identifies nearest vendor and coordinates emergency repair
4. Spend approval may happen verbally with Lindsay/Blake — Mike documents afterward
5. Lindsay processes payment and files documentation
