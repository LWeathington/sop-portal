# Daily Dispatch Objectives

## Key Objectives

1. Hunting for opportunities where we need them
   - Communicating to Customers our freight needs directly (Needs Email)
2. Building loads with all details entered
3. Entering Customers
   - Including setting up automated check calls
4. Entering locations
   - Including any notes we can add based on what we know, as well as updating customers as we learn things that we'd benefit from having in the system. Example: Knowing that they need a certain number that starts with _____.
5. Responding to dispatch emails in real time
6. Dispatching Drivers
   - Includes ensuring that the drivers pay has been calculated.
7. Communication with drivers (over communicate)
8. In route status checks
9. Tracking pay: Incentives and Deductions
10. Driver Support and Issue Resolution
11. Offer to or ask for help when the circumstances call for assistance

## First Principles

### Morning

1. **Compose and Send Morning Email to Brokers**
   - **Objective:** Inform brokers of your available capacity, specific needs, preferred routes, and any special considerations for today and the upcoming days.
   - **Details to Include:** Available trucks and their locations, targeted types of loads or industries, preferred routes, special capabilities, and any service offering or policy updates.

2. **Review Freight Status of loads we are currently hauling and Ditat updates**
   - Check the status of all active shipments
   - Update Ditat (including ensuring that each load has its correct paperwork for billing)
   - Communicate necessary items prioritizing those needing immediate attention or rerouting.

3. **Post / Refresh Needed Postings**
   - Update your load boards and freight platforms with your available capacity and specific load requirements for the day. Refresh these postings to maintain visibility.

5. **Communicate with Drivers**
   - Send automated morning check-ins to all drivers for updates and support any needs or issues they might have.

### Mid-to-Late Morning

6. **Compose and Send Follow-Up 'Freight Needs' Email to Brokers**
   - **Objective:** Update on capacity changes, reiterate urgent needs, and adjust preferences based on the morning's bookings and any new insights.

7. **Dispatch and Booking Management**
   - Continue to hunt for, earn and match loads with trucks efficiently, utilizing automated tools where possible for speed and accuracy.

### Throughout the Day

10. **Continuous Monitoring and Adjustment**
    - Keep an eye on incoming requests, market changes, and communications from brokers and clients to adjust operations as needed.

11. **Feedback Collection**
    - Encourage and collect feedback from drivers, clients, and brokers to refine services and communications.

### End of Day

12. **Review and Analyze End-of-Day Reports**
    - Assess operations with generated reports, focusing on completed shipments, pending loads, and any issues.

13. **Prepare for the Next Day**
    - Plan for the next day's shipments and set up automated reminders for critical tasks.

14. **Team Debrief**
    - Discuss the day's outcomes, highlight successes, and identify areas for improvement.
