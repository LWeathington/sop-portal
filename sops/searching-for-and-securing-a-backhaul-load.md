# Searching for and Securing a Backhaul Load

## Purpose

To identify, evaluate, and communicate backhaul load opportunities using approved load boards and email to optimize equipment utilization and reduce empty miles.

## Scope

Applies to assigned personnel responsible for searching for and securing backhaul freight.

## Responsible Party

Assigned personnel

## Systems Used

- Manifold
- DAT
- Gmail / Email system
- (Optional) Google Maps

## Procedure

### Step 1: Review Truck Availability and Needs

1.  Identify trucks requiring a backhaul based on:
    - Location
    - Available date and time
    - Equipment type
2.  Confirm destination preferences or constraints if applicable

### Step 2: Search for Backhaul Loads in Manifold

1.  Log into **Manifold**
2.  Navigate to the load search screen
3.  Enter search criteria, including:
    - Origin (current or projected truck location)
    - Destination or destination region
    - Date range
    - Equipment type
4.  Review available loads
5.  Identify potential backhaul candidates based on:
    - Rate
    - Distance
    - Timing
    - Compatibility with next load or plan

### Step 3: Search for Backhaul Loads in DAT

1.  Open **DAT**
2.  Navigate to load search
3.  Enter search criteria similar to Manifold:
    - Origin
    - Destination or destination region
    - Date
    - Equipment type
4.  Review posted loads
5.  Compare options against Manifold results

### Step 4: Evaluate Load Fit

For each potential backhaul, evaluate:
- Pickup and delivery windows
- Deadhead miles
- Rate vs. market
- Impact on next planned load
Discard loads that do not meet operational or timing requirements.

### Step 5: Contact Brokers via Email

1.  Open **Gmail**
2.  Locate existing broker threads or create a new email
3.  Request load details, including:
    - Rate confirmation
    - Pickup and delivery times
    - Commodity details
4.  Clearly identify:
    - Truck availability
    - Location
    - Desired timing

### Step 6: Monitor Responses and Negotiate

- Monitor email responses from brokers
- Clarify missing information
- Negotiate rate or timing as needed
- Track multiple conversations simultaneously

### Step 7: Select and Secure Backhaul

1.  Confirm the best backhaul option based on:
    - Rate
    - Timing
    - Fit with overall plan
2.  Accept the load verbally or via email as required
3.  Request written confirmation (rate confirmation)

### Step 8: Communicate Load Details Internally

1.  Notify dispatch of the secured backhaul
2.  Provide:
    - Broker name
    - Load details
    - Pickup and delivery information
3.  Coordinate system updates as needed

## Error Handling

- **No viable backhaul found**
  - Expand search radius or date range
  - Notify dispatch of empty miles risk
- **Broker non-responsive**
  - Follow up via email or phone
  - Continue searching alternative options
- **Timing conflict identified**
  - Do not commit to the load
  - Re-evaluate options

## Output

- A secured backhaul load **or**
- Clear documentation that no viable backhaul was available
