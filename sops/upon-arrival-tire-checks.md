# Upon Arrival: Tire Checks

## Purpose

To ensure all trucks and trailers are inspected daily for proper tire pressure, tire condition, cleanliness, and DOT compliance, and that all findings are accurately documented.

## Scope

This SOP applies to all personnel responsible for performing daily yard inspections of bobtail trucks and trailers at Spot On TMS facilities.

## Required Tools

- Tire pressure gauge
- Mobile device or computer
- Tire Inspection Sheet (Google Form)

## Inspection Location Rules

- Always start inspections on the east side of the building where most drivers park.

## Procedure — Preparation

1. Retrieve a tire gauge from the office.
2. Open the Tire Inspection Sheet using the link above.
3. Proceed to the east side of the building.

## Procedure — Bobtail Truck Inspections (East Side)

1. Start with the first bobtail truck.
2. Verify inspection is up to date and not due for DOT.
3. Inspect tires in this order:
   - Left steer
   - Right steer
   - Right-side drives
   - Left-side drives
4. Record PSI for each tire and inspect for damage or uneven wear.
5. Document issues in notes if found.
6. Repeat for all bobtail trucks.

## Procedure — Truck + Trailer Inspections (East Side)

1. Inspect both the truck and trailer at the same time if connected.
2. Verify DOT status for both units.
3. Record PSI and notes in the inspection sheet.

## Procedure — Trailer Inspections (East or West Side)

**Left Side Tires:**
- Left front outside
- Left front inside
- Left rear outside
- Left rear inside

**Rear Check:**
- If locked, check box (considered swept).
- If no lock, do not check box.
- Check cleanliness only if accessible.

**Right Side Tires:**
- Right rear outside
- Right rear inside
- Right front outside
- Right front inside

Record PSI and inspect all tires for damage or uneven wear.

## Completion

Ensure all trucks and trailers are inspected, all PSI values are recorded, DOT status is verified, and issues are documented before ending inspections.
