# Samsara Violation Daily Review

**Department:** Dispatch Operations / Compliance
**Owner:** Austin Boerckel
**Effective:** April 2026

---

## Overview

Austin reviews Samsara every morning for violations, faults, and alerts from the previous 24 hours. This is a non-negotiable part of the morning routine. Nothing gets dispatched until this review is complete.

---

## When to Run This

**Every morning, first thing upon arrival** — before dispatching any loads.

This review is part of the broader **Morning Dispatch Operations SOP** but is documented separately due to its compliance importance.

---

## Step 1: Log Into Samsara

1. Open **Samsara** (app or dashboard at cloud.samsara.com)
2. Navigate to the **Alerts** or **Safety** dashboard
3. Set the date filter to **Yesterday** (previous 24 hours)

---

## Step 2: Review HOS (Hours of Service) Violations

1. Click **Compliance** → **HOS Violations**
2. Look for:
   - **Hours remaining violations** (driver worked over their allowed hours)
   - **Recap violations**
   - **Unidentified driving** (truck moved without a driver logged in)
3. For each violation:
   - Identify the driver
   - Note the violation type and severity
   - Determine if the driver is still in a valid HOS window for today's dispatch

**If a driver has a serious HOS violation:** Do not dispatch until resolved. Contact the driver and document the issue.

---

## Step 3: Review DVIRs (Driver Vehicle Inspection Reports)

1. Click **Compliance** → **DVIRs**
2. Review all DVIRs submitted in the past 24 hours
3. Look for any **defects marked as unsafe** or **needs repair**
4. If a defect is flagged:
   - Do not dispatch the vehicle until the defect is cleared
   - Notify Mike Jackson immediately (see Fleet Maintenance Request Process SOP)
   - Log the defect and Mike's response

**Vehicles with open unsafe defects are NOT dispatched. No exceptions.**

---

## Step 4: Review Safety Events (Dashcam Alerts)

1. Click **Safety** → **Events**
2. Review any flagged events from the past 24 hours:
   - Harsh braking
   - Harsh cornering
   - Collision warnings
   - Distracted driving
   - Speeding violations

3. For each event:
   - Watch the clip if available
   - Determine if coaching is needed
   - Log the event in the driver's safety record

**Coaching approach:**
- First occurrence of minor event → mention it to the driver casually
- Repeated occurrences → formal coaching conversation, document in file
- Serious event (collision, near-miss, distracted driving) → call Blake immediately

---

## Step 5: Review Fault Codes

1. Click **Vehicles** → review the **Diagnostics / Fault Codes** section
2. Look for any new fault codes flagged overnight
3. Notify Mike Jackson of any new engine/system fault codes

---

## Step 6: Document and Act

After completing the review:

| Finding | Action |
|---------|--------|
| HOS violation | Document driver, violation type, and resolution |
| Unsafe DVIR defect | Pull vehicle, notify Mike, do not dispatch |
| Safety event requiring coaching | Note in driver file, coach within 24 hours |
| Fault code | Notify Mike via text same morning |
| All clear | Log "No violations — [date]" in the daily dispatch log |

---

## Step 7: Flag to Lindsay (If Needed)

If a violation could have compliance implications (DOT reportable accidents, ELD malfunctions, pattern of HOS violations), Austin flags it to Lindsay same day. Lindsay maintains the driver compliance file and tracks any violations requiring documentation.

---

## Driver Coaching Documentation

When a safety event requires coaching, Austin documents:
- Driver name
- Date of event
- Type of event
- What was discussed
- Driver acknowledgement

Lindsay files this in the driver's compliance folder.

---

## Red Flags That Go Straight to Blake

The following go to Blake **same day**, no waiting:
- Collision or accident (regardless of severity)
- ELD tampering or falsification suspicion
- Driver operating after HOS limit — repeatedly
- Unidentified driving that cannot be explained
- Any DOT-reportable incident
