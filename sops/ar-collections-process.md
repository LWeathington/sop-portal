# AR Collections Process (Accounts Receivable)

**Department:** Accounting & Finance
**Owner:** Lindsay Weathington
**Effective:** April 2026

---

## Overview

Lindsay owns all accounts receivable follow-up for Spot On Logistics. This SOP defines the process from invoice delivery through payment collection, including escalation steps when customers are past due.

---

## Step 1: Invoice Delivery

Invoices are sent via QuickBooks automated email immediately after the invoice batch is posted.

**Verify after each invoice batch:**
- All invoices have a valid customer email on file
- QB confirmation shows the email was sent
- Invoice amounts match what was agreed (rate × quantity)

If an email bounces, Lindsay contacts the customer directly to get updated billing contact info.

---

## Step 2: AR Aging Review Schedule

Lindsay reviews the AR aging report on the following schedule:

| Frequency | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Weekly (every Monday)** | Pull AR aging report from QB — review all open invoices |
| **30 days past due** | First follow-up contact |
| **45 days past due** | Second follow-up — escalate tone |
| **60 days past due** | Final notice — flag to Blake |
| **75+ days past due** | Blake decides: collections agency, hold service, or negotiate payment plan |

---

## Step 3: 30-Day Follow-Up

When an invoice hits 30 days past due:

1. Lindsay sends a **friendly reminder email** referencing the invoice number and amount
2. Lindsay confirms the customer received the original invoice
3. Lindsay confirms no disputes exist on the invoice (if dispute — see Step 6)

**Template:**
> "Hi [Name], I wanted to follow up on invoice #[XXXX] for $[amount] dated [date]. This invoice is now 30 days past due. Please let us know your expected payment date or if you have any questions. Thank you!"

---

## Step 4: 45-Day Follow-Up

1. Lindsay calls or emails with a more direct tone
2. Lindsay requests a **specific payment date commitment**
3. Lindsay notes the commitment in QB customer notes

**Template:**
> "Hi [Name], This is a follow-up on invoice #[XXXX] for $[amount], now 45 days past due. Please provide a payment date by [date]. We value our relationship and want to resolve this quickly."

---

## Step 5: 60-Day Flag to Blake

When an invoice hits 60 days past due:
1. Lindsay flags it to Blake in writing (email or Slack)
2. Lindsay provides: customer name, invoice #, amount, and history of contact attempts
3. Blake decides the next step (final notice, hold service, or payment plan)

---

## Step 6: Invoice Disputes

If a customer disputes an invoice:
1. Lindsay gets the dispute in writing (email preferred)
2. Lindsay verifies with Austin: Was the load completed as billed? POD on file?
3. If the dispute is valid → Lindsay issues a credit memo in QB and re-invoices correctly
4. If the dispute is not valid → Lindsay provides documentation (POD, rate confirmation) to the customer and re-requests payment

**Lindsay does not adjust an invoice without Austin's confirmation that the dispute is valid.**

---

## Step 7: Payment Posting

When payment is received:
1. Lindsay posts payment in QuickBooks against the correct invoice
2. Lindsay verifies the amount matches the invoice (flag short payments)
3. Lindsay sends a receipt confirmation if the customer requests one

**Short payments:** If a customer pays less than invoiced, Lindsay contacts them for the balance before closing the invoice. Do not close an invoice with an open balance.

---

## Step 8: Cash Flow Flag

If total AR over 45 days exceeds a threshold that impacts cash flow, Lindsay notifies Blake immediately — do not wait for the weekly review.

---

## Key Metrics Lindsay Tracks

| Metric | Goal |
|--------|------|
| Average days to collect | Under 35 days |
| Invoices over 60 days | Zero |
| Disputed invoices resolved | Within 5 business days |
