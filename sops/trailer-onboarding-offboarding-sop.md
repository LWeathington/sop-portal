# Trailer Onboarding / Offboarding

## Purpose

This SOP defines the process for adding trailers to the fleet (onboarding) and removing trailers from the fleet (offboarding).
The goal is to ensure accurate asset tracking, inspection compliance, and correct setup in Samsara and Ditat systems while maintaining proper financial records through Accounting.

## Trailer Identification Standards

All trailers must follow the company numbering system.
Roll Door Dry Vans → 200 series
Swing Door Dry Vans → 300 series
Unit numbers must appear on:
• Both sides of trailer
• Rear of trailer
• Samsara asset record
• Ditat trailer record
• Maintenance records

## Trailer Ownership Types

All trailers must be categorized as one of the following:
Owned -- Company owned asset
Leased -- Long term lease equipment
Rented -- Short term rental equipment
Accounting must record ownership type in the company asset register.

## Roles and Responsibilities

Operations:
• Trailer inspection
• Unit number assignment
• Samsara setup
• Ditat setup
• GPS installation
• Operational readiness
Accounting:
• Asset creation
• Lease tracking
• Depreciation tracking
• Insurance verification
• Title documentation

## Step 1 -- Physical Trailer Inspection (Before Acceptance)

Operations must perform a full inspection BEFORE onboarding the trailer into the fleet.
Structural Inspection
• Frame integrity
• Cross members
• Floor condition
• Roof condition
• Interior wall damage
• Rear impact guard (ICC bumper)
Door Inspection
Swing Doors (300 Series)
• Hinges
• Locking rods
• Door seals
• Alignment
• Latch operation
Roll Doors (200 Series)
• Track condition
• Roll mechanism
• Door spring tension
• Locking mechanism
• Smooth operation
Lighting Inspection
• Brake lights
• Turn signals
• Marker lights
• Clearance lights
• ABS indicator light
Tire and Wheel Inspection
• Tire tread depth
• Tire sidewall condition
• Tire pressure (must meet manufacturer PSI spec)
• Wheel cracks
• Lug nuts and studs
• Hub oil leaks
• Valve stems
Brake System
• Brake chambers
• Slack adjusters
• Air lines
• ABS wiring
• Brake pad wear
Electrical Inspection
• 7‑way connector condition
• Wiring harness damage
• Plug integrity
Safety Equipment
• DOT reflective tape
• Mud flaps
• Landing gear
• Glad hands
• Electrical cable condition
Documentation Verification
• VIN
• Plate number
• Registration
• Title or lease agreement
• Manufacturer
• Year of manufacture

## Step 2 -- Assign Trailer Number

After inspection approval Operations assigns the unit number.
Numbering Rules:
Roll Door → 200 series
Swing Door → 300 series
Apply unit numbers to:
• Both trailer sides
• Rear of trailer
• Internal asset tracking records

## Step 3 -- Install Samsara GPS via ABS Connection

Samsara Asset Gateway is installed using the trailer ABS wiring harness.
Installation Steps:
1. Verify ABS functionality (ABS light activates then shuts off normally)
2. Power off trailer and secure with wheel chocks
3. Locate 5‑pin ABS Weather‑Pack connector near axle
4. Insert Samsara cable inline between ABS connector and trailer wiring
5. Mount Asset Gateway on trailer nose or protected external location
6. Secure cables every 12‑18 inches
7. Restore power and confirm device LED status
Verify GPS reporting in Samsara dashboard.

## Step 4 -- Add Trailer to Samsara

Operations enters trailer information in the Samsara dashboard.
Navigation:
Samsara Dashboard → Assets → Trailers → Add Trailer
Required fields:
Trailer Name
Trailer Type (Dry Van)
Door Type (Swing or Roll)
Ownership Type
VIN
License Plate
Gateway Serial Number
Confirm trailer appears on Samsara map with active GPS tracking.

## Step 5 -- Add Trailer to Ditat

Operations creates the trailer record in Ditat.
Required fields:
Trailer Number
Trailer Type
Door Type
Ownership Type
VIN
Plate Number
Status = Active
Confirm the trailer is visible for dispatch equipment assignments.

## Step 6 -- Accounting Asset Setup

Accounting records the trailer asset.
Required fields:
Asset ID
Purchase price or lease terms
Ownership type
Depreciation status
Insurance coverage
Asset start date

## Step 7 -- Final Activation

Operations confirms the trailer is ready for use.
Checklist:
• Inspection completed
• Samsara tracking active
• Trailer visible in Ditat
• Unit numbers applied
• Documentation recorded
Trailer status is set to ACTIVE.

## Trailer Offboarding Process

Offboarding occurs when trailers are:
• Sold
• Lease returned
• Rental returned
• Total loss
• Scrapped

## Step 1 -- Trailer Recovery

Operations confirms the trailer has been returned to the yard and is empty and secured.

## Step 2 -- Offboarding Inspection

Operations documents trailer condition.
Check for:
• Structural damage
• Tire condition
• Door condition
• Missing components
• GPS hardware condition
Take photos and record inspection notes.

## Step 3 -- Remove Samsara Hardware

Remove Samsara Asset Gateway and cables.
Return hardware to equipment inventory for reuse.

## Step 4 -- Update Samsara

Mark trailer status as INACTIVE or RETIRED.
Recommended naming format:
RETired -- Trailer \### -- Date

## Step 5 -- Remove Trailer from Ditat

Operations updates trailer status to INACTIVE and removes it from dispatch equipment pools.

## Step 6 -- Accounting Asset Removal

Accounting performs final asset action.
Sold → record sale
Lease return → close lease
Scrapped → write off
Total loss → insurance claim
Asset status updated to DISPOSED.

## Monthly Fleet Audit (Recommended)

Operations performs monthly audit.
Verify:
• Samsara trailers match Ditat records
• Trailer numbers match decals
• GPS reporting active
• No missing equipment

## Records to Maintain

Each trailer must have the following records:
• Inspection reports
• Maintenance records
• Registration documents
• Ownership documentation
• GPS serial numbers
• Disposal records
