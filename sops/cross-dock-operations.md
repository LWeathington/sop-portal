# Cross-Dock Operations

**Department:** Sugar Creek / Fleet Operations
**Owner:** Mike Jackson
**Effective:** April 2026

---

## Overview

Mike Jackson owns cross-dock operations for Spot On Logistics. This SOP covers the coordination of freight that passes through a cross-dock facility — staging, transferring, and verifying loads without long-term storage.

---

## What Is a Cross-Dock?

A cross-dock is a facility where inbound freight from one truck is unloaded and loaded directly onto another truck — minimizing storage time. Spot On operates cross-dock functions primarily at the **Sugar Creek** facility.

---

## Step 1: Pre-Arrival Coordination

Before freight arrives at the cross-dock:

1. Mike confirms with Austin:
   - Inbound truck ETA and load details
   - Outbound truck availability and ETA
   - Any time-sensitive delivery windows on the outbound side
2. Mike ensures dock doors are available and clear
3. Mike confirms driver assignments for both inbound and outbound

**If outbound capacity is not confirmed before inbound arrival — Mike contacts Austin immediately.** Do not accept freight you cannot move.

---

## Step 2: Inbound Truck Arrival

When the inbound truck arrives:

1. Mike (or site contact) meets the driver at the dock
2. Verify the load against the **BOL (Bill of Lading):**
   - Piece count
   - Description of freight
   - Any temperature or handling requirements
3. Note the arrival time on the dock log
4. Unload freight and stage at the designated transfer area

**Discrepancy on arrival:** If piece count or freight description does not match the BOL, stop and contact Austin before unloading. Do not sign the BOL until confirmed.

---

## Step 3: Freight Staging

While waiting for the outbound truck:

1. Keep freight organized by outbound destination
2. Do not mix loads from different customers or destinations
3. Protect freight from weather and damage
4. Take photos of staged freight condition if there is any concern

---

## Step 4: Outbound Loading

When the outbound truck arrives:

1. Verify driver identity and load assignment with Austin
2. Load freight onto the outbound truck
3. Confirm piece count matches what was received
4. Complete the outbound BOL:
   - Shipper (Spot On or customer — confirm which)
   - Consignee name and address
   - Piece count and freight description
   - Driver and truck information
5. Obtain driver signature on outbound BOL
6. Note departure time on dock log

---

## Step 5: Documentation

After each cross-dock move, Mike ensures the following are documented:

| Document | Who Creates It | Where It Goes |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Inbound BOL (signed) | Driver provides | Mike files, sends to Lindsay |
| Outbound BOL (signed) | Mike creates/signs | Austin and Lindsay get copies |
| Dock log entry | Mike | Maintained at Sugar Creek |
| Any damage notes | Mike | Photos + written note to Austin + Lindsay |

Lindsay needs the BOLs to invoice correctly. **Send BOL copies to Lindsay the same day.**

---

## Step 6: Damage and Exceptions

If freight is damaged during cross-dock:

1. Stop — do not continue loading damaged freight
2. Take photos immediately
3. Notify Austin and Blake same day
4. Mike documents the condition in writing
5. Do not release freight to the outbound driver until Austin and Blake decide how to handle

---

## Step 7: End of Day Dock Log Review

Mike reviews the dock log at the end of each day:
- All inbound and outbound moves accounted for
- BOLs collected and sent to Lindsay
- Any open items flagged to Austin
