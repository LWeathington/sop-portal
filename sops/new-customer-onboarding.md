# New Customer Onboarding — Sales to Operations Handoff

**Department:** Sales / Dispatch / Accounting
**Owner:** Tim Utzinger (sales close) → Austin Boerckel (ops setup) → Lindsay Weathington (billing setup)
**Effective:** April 2026

---

## Overview

When Tim closes a new customer, there is a defined handoff process to ensure operations and accounting are set up before the first load runs. Nothing should fall through the cracks between "deal closed" and "first invoice sent."

---

## Trigger

This process begins when Tim confirms a customer has signed or verbally committed to moving freight with Spot On.

---

## Phase 1: Tim — Sales Close & Handoff Package

**Tim completes all of the following before calling the handoff complete:**

### Step 1: Finalize Customer Details
- Customer legal name (as it will appear on invoices)
- Primary operations contact (name, phone, email)
- Primary billing/AP contact (name, phone, email — may be different person)
- Physical address and billing address

### Step 2: Document Agreed Rates
- Lane(s) and agreed rate(s) per lane
- Equipment type required (dry van, flatbed, etc.)
- Any accessorial charges agreed to (fuel surcharge formula, detention rate, etc.)
- Payment terms (Net 30 is standard — flag anything different to Lindsay)

### Step 3: Document Operational Requirements
- Pick-up and delivery locations
- Standard appointment windows
- Special handling, loading, or equipment requirements
- Frequency (loads per week or month)
- Who to call if there are service issues (customer's ops contact)

### Step 4: Send Handoff Email
Tim sends a **Handoff Email** to Austin and Lindsay (CC Blake) containing all of the above.

**Subject line format:** `New Customer Onboarding — [Customer Name] — Start Date: [Date]`

---

## Phase 2: Lindsay — Billing Setup

**Lindsay completes within 2 business days of receiving the handoff email:**

### Step 1: Create Customer in QuickBooks
1. Add new customer record in QB
2. Enter: legal name, billing contact, billing email, billing address
3. Set payment terms (per Tim's handoff — default Net 30)
4. Save

### Step 2: Verify Rate Setup
1. Confirm the rates in Tim's handoff match what Austin will build loads at in Ditat
2. Flag any discrepancies to Tim before the first load runs
3. Confirm the billing email is correct — send a test if unsure

### Step 3: Set Up Invoice Template (if needed)
Some customers require specific invoice formats or reference numbers. Lindsay notes any special requirements in the QB customer record.

### Step 4: Confirm Back to Tim and Austin
Lindsay replies to the handoff email confirming billing is set up and ready.

---

## Phase 3: Austin — Operations Setup

**Austin completes within 2 business days of receiving the handoff email:**

### Step 1: Confirm Capacity
Austin confirms Spot On has the capacity to service the lanes and frequencies agreed to. If there is a concern, Austin flags to Blake and Tim immediately — before the first load is promised.

### Step 2: Set Up Customer in Ditat
1. Add customer in Ditat TMS if not already present
2. Confirm customer reference numbers and load-building requirements
3. Set up any recurring load templates if the freight is consistent

### Step 3: Brief the Drivers (if applicable)
If the customer has specific requirements (dock procedures, PPE, etc.), Austin briefs the assigned driver(s) before the first load.

### Step 4: First Load
Austin assigns and dispatches the first load. Austin monitors closely on the first 2-3 loads to ensure smooth execution.

### Step 5: Confirm Back to Tim
Austin replies to the handoff email confirming ops is ready and the first load is dispatched.

---

## Phase 4: Tim — Follow-Up

**Tim follows up with the customer within 5 business days of the first load:**
- Was the service what they expected?
- Any issues on the first load?
- What's the plan for the next load?

Tim documents the feedback and shares any operational issues with Austin.
