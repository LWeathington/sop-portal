# Equipment Repair Approval Workflow

**Department:** Fleet Maintenance / Accounting
**Owner:** Mike Jackson (request + execution) + Lindsay Weathington (approval + paperwork)
**Effective:** April 2026

---

## Overview

Every fleet repair requires the right level of approval before work is authorized. This SOP defines the approval tiers, the process for each, and the documentation required after completion.

---

## Approval Tiers

| Estimated Cost | Who Approves | Turnaround |
|----------------|-------------|-----------|
| **Under $500** | Mike Jackson (auto-approve) | Same day |
| **$500 – $2,500** | Lindsay Weathington | Within 24 hours |
| **Over $2,500** | Blake Lappan | Within 48 hours |

---

## Tier 1: Under $500 — Mike Auto-Approves

1. Mike evaluates the issue and gets a quote from the shop
2. Mike authorizes the repair directly — no additional approval needed
3. Mike sends the completed invoice to Lindsay after the repair is done
4. Lindsay processes payment and files the receipt

**Examples:** Oil change, minor tire repair, small parts replacement, fluid top-off.

---

## Tier 2: $500–$2,500 — Lindsay Approval

### Mike's Request to Lindsay
Mike contacts Lindsay (text or email) with:
- Truck or trailer unit number
- Description of the issue
- Shop name and contact
- Quoted cost and estimated completion date
- Whether the truck is currently out of service

**Template Mike can use:**
> "Unit #[X] — [issue description]. Quote from [shop name]: $[amount]. ETA [date]. Truck [is/is not] out of service. Ok to proceed?"

### Lindsay's Decision
Lindsay will:
1. Check current cash flow position
2. Approve or ask for a delay based on timing
3. Respond to Mike within **24 hours** (same day if truck is out of service)

### If Lindsay Approves
Mike proceeds with the repair and provides completed invoice to Lindsay when done.

### If Lindsay Requests Delay
Mike coordinates with Austin on whether the truck can remain in service or must be sidelined. Mike revisits timing with Lindsay within the agreed window.

---

## Tier 3: Over $2,500 — Blake Approval

### Mike's Request to Blake (131 Rule)
Mike presents to Blake using the **131 Rule:**
- **1 Problem:** What's broken and what's the impact?
- **3 Solutions:** At least 3 options (e.g., repair at Shop A, repair at Shop B, defer and use backup unit)
- **1 Recommendation:** Mike's recommended path with reasoning

Mike also includes:
- Quotes (at least 2 when possible for amounts over $2,500)
- Impact on fleet availability
- Timeline

### Blake's Decision
Blake responds within **48 hours**. If the truck is out of service and impacting revenue, Blake prioritizes faster.

### If Blake Approves
Mike proceeds. Lindsay still processes the payment and files the documentation.

---

## After Every Repair — Documentation Required

Regardless of approval tier, after every repair is complete:

**Mike sends Lindsay:**
- Repair invoice/receipt from the shop
- Brief description of what was done
- Truck/trailer unit number
- Date of completion

**Lindsay files:**
- Invoice in vehicle's compliance folder (Drive + physical)
- Payment in QuickBooks under fleet maintenance expense
- Updates fleet maintenance log
- Reviews if the repair triggers any registration or compliance updates (e.g., major engine work → update insurance)

---

## Key Rule

**No repair is authorized without the proper approval level being obtained first.**

If a driver or anyone else attempts to authorize a repair directly, Mike must be notified immediately and brought into the process. Austin does not authorize repairs without Mike.
