# Sales Discovery Process

**Department:** Sales
**Owner:** Tim Utzinger — Sales Director
**Effective:** April 2026

---

## Overview

The Discovery process is the most critical step in the Spot On sales cycle. **No pricing is given until a discovery summary is completed.** This protects our margins, ensures we understand the customer's real needs, and gives Tim the information needed to price effectively.

---

## Sales Lifecycle at a Glance

```
Awareness → Lead → Qualify → Discovery → Solution → Pricing → Contract → Onboard
```

---

## Step 1: Lead Identification & Qualification

**Tim owns this step.**

A lead qualifies for the discovery process if:
- They have consistent, repeatable freight (not one-time moves)
- They are in a lane or vertical we can serve profitably
- They have expressed genuine interest (responded to outreach, attended a call, or made an inbound inquiry)

**Disqualify early if:** Freight is too unpredictable, margin is impossible, or the prospect is just price-shopping with no intent.

---

## Step 2: Schedule the Discovery Meeting

1. Tim schedules a call or in-person meeting with the prospect
2. Tim prepares by reviewing:
   - Their industry and freight profile
   - Current carrier relationships (if known)
   - Our capacity in their lanes
3. Tim notifies Blake if the account could be a large or strategic opportunity

---

## Step 3: Conduct the Discovery Meeting

The goal of discovery is to understand the customer's operation deeply before we ever discuss price.

**Key questions Tim must answer during discovery:**

| Category | Questions to Ask |
|----------|-----------------|
| Freight Profile | What are your typical lanes? Frequency per week/month? |
| Equipment | What trailer types do you need? Any special requirements? |
| Timing | What pick-up and delivery windows are required? |
| Pain Points | What problems do you have with your current carriers? |
| Decision Process | Who else is involved in the decision? Timeline? |
| Volume | What's the minimum and maximum volume per month? |
| Pricing History | What are you currently paying? (Get a ballpark if possible) |

---

## Step 4: Complete the Discovery Summary (REQUIRED)

**Before any pricing can be sent, Tim must complete a Discovery Summary document.**

The Discovery Summary includes:
- Customer name and contact
- Primary lanes (origin → destination)
- Equipment type(s) needed
- Freight frequency (loads per week/month)
- Delivery windows and any special requirements
- Current carrier situation and pain points
- Estimated volume and revenue potential
- Tim's recommended approach and rate range

**If a Discovery Summary is not completed, pricing cannot be submitted. No exceptions.**

---

## Step 5: Involve Blake If Needed

Tim involves Blake when:
- The account is niche or requires special pricing authority
- The volume would significantly impact fleet capacity
- Partnership-level pricing (long-term contract, volume commitment) is being considered

Tim presents using the **131 Rule:** 1 Problem, 3 Solutions, 1 Recommendation.

---

## Step 6: Build and Present the Solution

1. Tim uses the Rate Guide and Profit Calculator to build pricing
2. Tim prepares a proposal (standard or custom, based on account size)
3. Tim presents to the prospect — walk through the solution, not just the rate
4. Tim handles objections and negotiates within approved authority

---

## Step 7: Contract and Close

1. Tim finalizes terms with the customer
2. Tim submits contract to Blake for signature (if above standard terms)
3. Tim confirms start date with customer
4. Tim hands off to Austin for operational onboarding (see New Customer Onboarding SOP)

---

## Step 8: Sales → Operations Handoff

Tim provides Austin and Lindsay with:
- Customer name, contact, and billing info
- Agreed lanes and rates
- Equipment requirements
- Service start date
- Any special instructions or commitments made

Lindsay sets up the customer in QuickBooks. Austin confirms operational capacity and schedules first loads.
