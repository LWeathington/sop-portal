# Tag Renewal & Registration Tracking

**Department:** Accounting & Finance / Compliance
**Owner:** Lindsay Weathington
**Effective:** April 2026

---

## Overview

Lindsay owns all vehicle tag renewals, registration tracking, and related compliance paperwork for the Spot On fleet. This process ensures no truck runs with expired tags and all registration documents are current.

---

## Vehicle Tracking Log

Lindsay maintains a master spreadsheet tracking:

| Column | What to Track |
|--------|--------------|
| Unit # | Truck or trailer number |
| VIN | Vehicle identification number |
| License Plate | Current plate number |
| State | Registration state |
| Expiration Date | Tag/registration expiration |
| Renewal Cost | Expected renewal fee |
| Renewal Status | Pending / Submitted / Complete |
| Notes | Any flags or exceptions |

**Update this log any time a renewal is completed or a new vehicle is added.**

---

## Step 1: 90-Day Advance Review

90 days before any tag expiration:
1. Lindsay reviews the master tracking log at the start of each month
2. Flags any registrations expiring within 90 days
3. Begins renewal process (see Step 2)

**Set a recurring calendar reminder on the 1st of every month to review the log.**

---

## Step 2: Initiate Renewal

**For standard state registrations:**
1. Log into the applicable state DMV portal or contact the renewal service
2. Confirm current insurance is on file (required for renewal)
3. Confirm current DOT number and USDOT authority are active
4. Submit renewal and pay fee
5. Save confirmation receipt

**For IFTA (fuel tax registration):**
- Renewal is annual — contact accountant or handle via IFTA online portal
- Coordinate with Blake for payment authorization

---

## Step 3: Receive and File Documents

When renewed documents arrive (mail or digital):
1. Upload digital copy to the vehicle's folder in Google Drive
   - Path: `Fleet > [Unit #] > Registration`
2. Place physical copy in the vehicle's glovebox
3. Update master tracking log — mark "Complete" and enter new expiration date
4. Confirm with Austin that the physical copy is in the truck

---

## Step 4: New Vehicle Added

When Mike or Blake adds a new vehicle to the fleet:
1. Mike notifies Lindsay with: VIN, year/make/model, purchase date
2. Lindsay obtains title and previous registration from Mike
3. Lindsay initiates new registration in the appropriate state
4. Lindsay adds vehicle to master tracking log
5. Lindsay adds vehicle to insurance policy — contact insurance agent

---

## Step 5: Expired or Flagged Tags

If a tag is discovered to be expired or about to expire:
1. Notify Austin immediately — truck should NOT be dispatched until resolved
2. Begin emergency renewal process same day
3. Contact Blake if the truck must be sidelined and a replacement is needed

---

## Reminders Schedule

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Monthly (1st of month) | Review full tag log |
| 90 days out | Flag and start renewal process |
| 30 days out | Confirm renewal is in progress |
| 7 days out | Escalate if not yet renewed — contact Blake |
| Day of expiration | Truck must be sidelined if not resolved |
