What's Changed

A summary of the role changes and new processes that came out of our Business Process Management journey. Use this as a reference going forward.

Why we did this: We went through a BPM (Business Process Management) review as a team to figure out who truly owns what. The result is a set of clear role boundaries, documented processes, and this SOP portal — so nothing falls through the cracks and everyone knows exactly who to go to for what.

The goal: When something goes wrong, we know who's accountable. When something needs to get done, we know who does it. This is not about blame — it's about clarity.

By Person — What Changed For You
🔧
Mike Jackson
Fleet Maintenance Manager — NEW ROLE
⭐
New Role
Mike is now the official Fleet Maintenance Manager for all Spot On equipment. Fleet first, always.
✅
Mon–Thu (On-Site) — Primary Focus
Fleet maintenance and repairs own the day. Mike evaluates every issue, gets quotes, coordinates with vendors, and schedules all work. Auto-approves under $500. After fleet tasks are handled, Mike works on Automation & AI projects as secondary focus.
🏠
Friday — Work From Home
Mike works remotely on Fridays. He is required to check in with Sugar Creek to make sure operations are running smoothly and no one needs help. Available by phone for any fleet emergencies.
🚫
No Longer Mike's
Sugar Creek daily site management and cross-dock operations — those are now Steve Weindorfer's responsibility. Mike supports Steve on fleet/equipment issues flagged at the site, but does not run the facility.
📌
Spend Boundary — Still Applies
Spend over $500 requires Lindsay's approval. Over $2,500 requires Blake. Mike gets quotes and presents — does not authorize large spend unilaterally.
🏭
Steve Weindorfer
Sugar Creek Lead → Sugar Creek Manager — PROMOTED
⭐
Promoted to Manager
Steve was previously the Sugar Creek Lead — handling hands-on night operations, mentoring new drivers, and acting as the Level 1 on-site contact for immediate issues. He is now the Sugar Creek Manager, inheriting full site management from Mike Jackson who moved to Fleet Maintenance.
✅
Now Owns — Scheduling & Compliance
Steve ensures all shifts are covered and that Paychex daily schedules match operational needs. He audits driver time cards and verifies PTO/time-off requests.
✅
Now Owns — Cross-Dock Oversight
Steve oversees all cross-dock operations — arrival/departure photos, seal verification, cargo condition checks, vendor coordination, and warehouse-related payments.
✅
Now Owns — Safety Leadership
Steve conducts weekly safety briefs and performs yard safety walks to ensure PPE and protocol compliance. He is required to report equipment failures and property damage within 15 minutes. He is now the Level 2 incident escalation contact (was Level 1 as Lead).
✅
Now Owns — Daily Reporting to Austin
Steve submits daily operational summaries to Austin Boerckel and communicates yard delays, inbound/outbound workflow crises, and any equipment issues to leadership. He also performs physical yard walkthroughs cross-referenced against Samsara to verify equipment location and condition.
📌
Equipment Still Goes to Mike
Steve flags equipment and fleet issues at Sugar Creek, but Mike Jackson owns the repair coordination. Steve → Mike → repair process. Steve does not authorize shop work.
🚛
Austin Boerckel
Operations Manager / Dispatch
🚫
Removed from Austin's Plate
Fleet maintenance and repairs — Austin flags equipment issues but does NOT coordinate repairs or authorize shop work. That's Mike now.
🚫
Removed from Austin's Plate
Cross-dock operations — Steve Weindorfer owns this now. Austin supports with driver coordination only.
📌
Clarified — Still Austin's
All dispatch, load assignment, driver compliance (HOS/Samsara), freight booking, trip status updates, and the daily morning/afternoon routines. This is Austin's world.
✅
Formalized
Samsara daily violation review is now a documented daily routine — every morning before dispatch. DVIRs, HOS violations, safety events.
💼
Lindsay Weathington
Accounting Manager
✅
Added to Lindsay's Plate
Tag renewals and vehicle registration — Lindsay tracks all expiration dates 90 days out and manages renewals. No truck runs with an expired tag.
✅
Added to Lindsay's Plate
Maintenance spend approval ($500–$2,500) — Mike brings quotes to Lindsay for a cash flow check before authorizing mid-range repairs.
✅
Formalized Under Lindsay
Driver compliance files — Lindsay creates and maintains the compliance folder for every driver. Policy forms must be signed before a driver is dispatched.
📌
Clarified Boundary
Lindsay handles the paperwork side of fleet operations. Mike handles the physical side. They work together — Mike sends docs, Lindsay files and pays.
📈
Tim Utzinger
Sales Director
✅
New Rule — Formal
Discovery summary required before pricing. No rates go out without a completed discovery document. This protects margin and ensures we understand what we're quoting.
✅
New Process
Formal handoff to Austin and Lindsay when a customer is closed — handoff email with full customer details, rates, and start date. Operations doesn't start without it.
📌
Clarified
Tim follows up with the customer within 5 days of their first load. If there are operational issues, Austin hears about it through Tim first.
🏗️
Phil Stuart
KC Yard Manager
📌
Clarified — No Major Changes
Phil's role is largely unchanged — KC yard, Cargill spotting, Empire operations, driver coordination on site. His responsibilities are now formally documented.
✅
New Connection
Equipment issues Phil observes at KC now go to Mike Jackson (not Austin) for maintenance coordination. Phil → Mike → repair process.
👔
Blake Lappan
CEO / Owner
📌
Goal — Get Blake Out of the Middle
The BPM goal was to reduce how many things land on Blake's desk that others can handle. Each role now has clear ownership so the team can execute without Blake in every decision.
📌
Blake Still Owns
Final approval on: hire/fire, repair spend over $2,500, equipment investment, rate exceptions, niche pricing, and any escalations the team can't resolve.
✅
131 Rule — How to Come to Blake
When the team needs Blake's decision, present it using the 131 Rule: 1 Problem, 3 Solutions, 1 Recommendation. This keeps decisions moving and shows the work was done.
